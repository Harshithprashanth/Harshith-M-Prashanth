## 🧑‍💻 About Me

I am a dedicated Electronics and Communication Engineering graduate currently pursuing my M.Tech in Data Science, with a deep passion for Research and Development at the convergence of intelligent systems, language models, and physical signal intelligence. My academic and professional journey has evolved from hardware-level circuit design and digital signal processing through machine learning and into the cutting-edge domain of Large Language Models (LLMs) and LLMOps engineering.

Over the past several years I have built extensive hands-on experience designing and deploying production-grade LLM pipelines encompassing retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems, instruction-tuned and parameter-efficient fine-tuned models (LoRA, QLoRA), multi-agent orchestration frameworks, and end-to-end LLMOps workflows covering experiment tracking, evaluation, monitoring, and deployment. I work fluently across the full stack from transformer architecture internals to cloud and edge deployment patterns.

My research lies at a compelling triple intersection: the hardware-aware thinking cultivated through ECE — understanding resource constraints, signal fidelity, and real-time processing — combined with the rigorous statistical and analytical foundations of data science, and the powerful reasoning and generative capabilities of modern LLMs. This perspective enables me to design intelligent systems that are simultaneously theoretically grounded, computationally efficient, and practically deployable on real-world infrastructure including edge and IoT devices.

I am particularly drawn to questions that few researchers are positioned to explore: Can LLMs reason about the physical characteristics of signals? How do we adapt foundation models to engineering and scientific domains without sacrificing generalization? What architectural choices and quantization strategies allow large models to run on resource-constrained embedded systems? How should production LLM pipelines be instrumented, evaluated, and guarded in safety-critical applications?

My vision for R&D encompasses LLMs for scientific domain adaptation, efficient LLMOps infrastructure, intelligent signal processing augmented by language model reasoning, edge-deployable TinyLLMs, and IoT systems empowered by natural language interfaces. I am actively seeking research collaborations, PhD opportunities, and industry R&D positions where these interdisciplinary strengths can be applied to high-impact problems.

> *"The most interesting research problems live at the boundaries of disciplines — where hardware meets intelligence, where signals become knowledge, and where language models bridge human intent and physical systems."*

## 🔬 Research Interests

### 🤖 1. Large Language Models & Foundation Models

My primary research focus centres on the science and engineering of large language models. I investigate transformer architectures — attention mechanisms, positional encodings, tokenization strategies, and empirical scaling laws — alongside advanced fine-tuning paradigms including full fine-tuning, LoRA, QLoRA, and other PEFT methods. I study instruction tuning, RLHF, Constitutional AI, and DPO for model alignment, and maintain an active interest in efficient inference through quantization, pruning, and knowledge distillation. A key thrust is domain-specific LLM adaptation for engineering and scientific literature.

### ⚙️ 2. LLMOps & Production AI Systems

I research and build end-to-end LLM pipeline infrastructure: RAG system architecture and retrieval optimization, vector database selection and indexing, LLM monitoring and observability, prompt management and versioning, cost and latency optimization, and safety guardrails. I am particularly interested in the engineering challenges of moving LLM research into reliable production systems and the evaluation frameworks needed to measure real-world performance.

### 🔬 3. Machine Learning & Deep Learning

Research in neural network architectures for structured and unstructured data, with emphasis on signal-domain applications. Multi-modal learning combining sensor time-series, images, and text. Representation learning and transfer learning strategies for low-data engineering domains.

### 📡 4. Signal Processing & Signal Intelligence

Application of classical and learned signal processing to intelligent analysis problems. DSP algorithm design, spectral analysis, time-frequency representations, and the integration of deep learning into signal processing pipelines. Wireless communications, biomedical signal analysis (ECG, EEG), and industrial vibration/fault detection.

### 🔌 5. Edge Computing & Embedded AI

TinyML and on-device inference for resource-constrained microcontrollers and SBCs. Hardware-software co-design for efficient neural network deployment. Quantization and distillation strategies for edge LLM inference. Real-time AI on Raspberry Pi, STM32, and FPGA platforms.

### 📊 6. Data Science & Analytics

Statistical modelling, feature engineering, time-series forecasting, and predictive analytics on large heterogeneous datasets. Experimental design and rigorous empirical evaluation methodology.

### 🌐 7. IoT & Intelligent Sensing Systems

Sensor fusion, MQTT-based data pipelines, anomaly detection in industrial IoT streams, and LLM-powered natural language interfaces for IoT systems. Predictive maintenance and smart monitoring systems.

### 🤖 Project 1: Domain-Adaptive RAG System for Scientific Literature

**Domain**: LLMOps + RAG + NLP

Production-grade retrieval-augmented generation system designed for scientific and engineering literature. The pipeline ingests PDFs and arXiv papers, applies semantic chunking with sentence-transformers, stores embeddings in ChromaDB, and serves queries through a fine-tuned LLM backend. Advanced retrieval techniques include Hypothetical Document Embeddings (HyDE), cross-encoder re-ranking, and multi-step query decomposition.

**Tech Stack**: Python · LangChain · ChromaDB · Sentence-Transformers · OpenAI API · HuggingFace · RAGAS · FastAPI · Docker · LangSmith

**Results**: 🎯 Faithfulness: 0.91 | Answer Relevancy: 0.88 | Context Precision: 0.85

---

### ⚙️ Project 2: LLM Fine-Tuning Pipeline for Technical Domain Adaptation

**Domain**: LLMOps + LLM Fine-tuning

End-to-end QLoRA fine-tuning pipeline for Llama-2 and Mistral-7B on technical domain datasets. Implements 4-bit quantization via BitsAndBytes with gradient checkpointing and LoRA adapter training. Pipeline covers dataset preparation, instruction formatting (Alpaca/ChatML templates), training, evaluation, GGUF conversion, and Ollama deployment.

**Tech Stack**: PyTorch · HuggingFace PEFT · TRL · BitsAndBytes · W&B · MLflow · Ollama · LangChain

**Results**: 🎯 65% VRAM reduction · 97% of full fine-tuning performance · 3.2× inference speedup post-quantization

---

### 🕸️ Project 3: Multi-Agent LLM Orchestration Framework

**Domain**: LLMOps + Agentic AI

Multi-agent research automation system built on LangChain and LangGraph. Specialized agents handle literature review (RAG-powered), automated data analysis (code interpreter), document summarization, citation management, and structured report generation. Implements ReAct reasoning patterns with arXiv API, Semantic Scholar, and Python REPL tool integrations. Full observability via LangSmith.

**Tech Stack**: LangChain · LangGraph · LangSmith · OpenAI API · Claude API · FastAPI · Redis · Docker

**Results**: 🎯 78% reduction in manual research workflow time

---

### 🌐 Project 4: LLM-Powered IoT Anomaly Detection & Natural Language Interface

**Domain**: LLMOps + IoT + Edge AI

Deploys quantized Mistral-7B via Ollama on an edge server connected to an industrial IoT sensor network. A RAG pipeline injects real-time sensor context into prompts, allowing field operators to query system state, anomalies, predicted failures, and maintenance schedules in plain English. Function calling enables direct actuator control through natural language commands.

**Tech Stack**: Ollama · Mistral-7B · LangChain · MQTT · InfluxDB · Grafana · Raspberry Pi · FastAPI · ChromaDB

**Results**: 🎯 Sub-2-second response latency on edge hardware · 93% query accuracy

---

### 📊 Project 5: LLM Evaluation & Benchmarking Suite
