## 🌐 Research Vision

> *"My research programme seeks to create a new class of intelligent systems that combine the reasoning power of large language models with the grounded understanding of physical signals and the engineering rigour of embedded computing. The confluence of these domains is not merely additive — it is multiplicative, enabling capabilities that neither field can achieve alone."*

The history of artificial intelligence has been marked by successive waves of integration: symbolic reasoning gave way to statistical learning, which gave way to deep learning, which is now being transformed by foundation models and large language models. Each transition has not merely replaced the previous paradigm but has absorbed and extended it, incorporating its strengths while transcending its limitations.

My research vision is premised on the belief that the next major integration is the fusion of LLM reasoning with physical signal intelligence — the ability of AI systems to understand, interpret, and act upon the raw data streams generated by the physical world. Industrial machinery, biological organisms, wireless networks, and environmental sensors all communicate through signals. Today's LLMs are trained almost exclusively on text and, to a lesser extent, images. They lack the ability to reason directly about vibration signatures, ECG rhythms, spectral anomalies, or RF channel characteristics.

I aim to build the theoretical and practical foundations for **Physics-Aware Language Models** (PALMs) — foundation models that natively understand physical signals, can reason about their properties in natural language, and can act on that understanding through tool use, code generation, and direct interface with sensing and control systems. This vision integrates my expertise in ECE, data science, and LLM engineering into a coherent long-term research agenda.

The practical implications span healthcare (LLM-assisted ECG/EEG interpretation), industrial automation (NL interfaces to factory floor sensor networks), environmental monitoring (LLM reasoning over multi-modal environmental data), and wireless communications (intelligent spectrum management through language model reasoning).

## 🤖 Area 1: LLMs for Scientific & Engineering Domains

### Motivation

General-purpose LLMs — even state-of-the-art models like GPT-4 and Claude Opus — exhibit significant performance gaps when applied to specialized engineering and scientific domains. Trained predominantly on web-scale general text, these models lack deep familiarity with domain-specific notation, experimental protocols, equipment terminology, and the mathematical formalism of specialized fields such as digital signal processing, VLSI design, control theory, and RF engineering. Their outputs in these domains frequently contain plausible-sounding but technically incorrect statements — a particularly dangerous failure mode in engineering applications.

### Research Questions

1. **Efficient Fine-tuning for Technical Domains**: What LoRA rank, alpha, and target module configurations yield optimal domain adaptation for engineering texts? How do QLoRA quantization levels affect domain-specific knowledge retention versus general capability preservation?
2. **Optimal RAG Architecture for Scientific Literature**: How should scientific papers be chunked, embedded, and retrieved to maximize faithfulness while minimizing context-length overhead? What are the trade-offs between dense retrieval, sparse (BM25) retrieval, and hybrid approaches on technical question-answering benchmarks?
3. **LLMs for Signal Processing Algorithm Design**: Can instruction-tuned models generate correct DSP algorithm implementations from natural language specifications? What training data characteristics most improve performance on this task?
4. **Engineering-Specific Evaluation**: What metrics and benchmark tasks best capture the engineering-domain competence of an LLM? How can we construct evaluation sets that test technical reasoning rather than pattern matching?

### Proposed Methods

- **Instruction Tuning**: Curate domain-specific instruction-following datasets from engineering textbooks, technical standards, research papers, and laboratory documentation; apply SFT followed by DPO alignment
- **Domain RAG Pipelines**: Develop and compare chunking strategies (fixed-size, semantic, structure-aware), embedding models (domain-specific vs. general), and retrieval augmentation configurations using the RAGAS evaluation framework
- **LLM-as-Judge Evaluation**: Leverage GPT-4 as an automated judge with carefully engineered evaluation rubrics calibrated against expert human judgements
- **Benchmark Construction**: Develop the **EngLLM-Bench** benchmark — a systematic evaluation suite covering signal processing, embedded systems, circuit analysis, and communication theory

### Applications

- Automated technical report generation from sensor data
- Literature review assistants for engineering researchers
- LLM-based code debugging and documentation for DSP and embedded C/C++
- Natural language query interfaces for engineering simulation software
- Intelligent textbook tutoring systems for ECE students

## ⚙️ Area 2: LLMOps & Efficient Production Deployment

### Motivation

There exists a significant gap between LLM capabilities demonstrated in research settings and the reliable, observable, cost-efficient, and safe deployment of those capabilities in production systems. This gap is the domain of LLMOps — the engineering discipline of designing, deploying, monitoring, and continuously improving production LLM applications. Despite its practical importance, LLMOps remains relatively underexplored as a formal research area, with most literature focusing on model capabilities rather than deployment infrastructure.

### Research Questions

1. **RAG Pipeline Optimization**: What combination of retrieval strategy, re-ranking, and context assembly maximizes end-to-end RAG performance on domain-specific question-answering while minimizing latency and cost?
2. **Hallucination Minimization in Safety-Critical Applications**: What architectural guardrails, retrieval augmentation strategies, and post-generation verification methods are most effective at reducing harmful hallucinations in industrial and healthcare LLM applications?
3. **Production Monitoring Metrics**: What observable signals most reliably indicate degradation in LLM application quality over time? How should alert thresholds be calibrated to minimize false positives while catching genuine distribution shift?
4. **Edge LLM Deployment**: What is the Pareto frontier of model quality versus latency versus memory footprint for quantized LLMs on ARM Cortex-A and RISC-V edge hardware?

### Proposed Methods

- Systematic comparison of retrieval strategies (HyDE, multi-query, contextual compression, re-ranking) with controlled ablations on domain-specific QA benchmarks
- Implementation and evaluation of Constitutional AI, NeMo Guardrails, and Guardrails AI frameworks for hallucination prevention
- Design and evaluation of automated production monitoring pipelines using LangSmith and custom metric dashboards
- Empirical benchmarking of GGUF-quantized models (Q4_K_M, Q5_K_M, Q8_0) on Raspberry Pi 4/5 and Jetson Nano platforms with systematic latency, throughput, and quality measurements

### Contributions

- Open-source LLMOps evaluation toolkit for domain-specific RAG applications
- Best practice guidelines for LLM deployment in industrial and healthcare settings
- Empirical benchmark comparing edge LLM inference frameworks (Ollama, llama.cpp, MLC-LLM, TFLite)

## 📡 Area 3: Intelligent Signal Processing with LLMs

### Motivation

Signal processing and language modelling have evolved almost entirely independently, yet they share deep mathematical connections. Both deal fundamentally with sequence modelling, pattern recognition, and information extraction. The transformer's attention mechanism was originally motivated by sequence transduction problems in NLP, but its core mathematical operation — computing weighted sums of value vectors using query-key similarity scores — applies with equal validity to signal sequences. I hypothesize that large language models, properly adapted, can learn to reason meaningfully about physical signal characteristics — not through raw sample processing, but through intermediate representations that bridge physical and linguistic domains.

### Research Questions

1. **Can LLMs Reason About Signal Characteristics?** Given a text description of a signal's spectral properties, time-domain characteristics, and noise profile, can fine-tuned LLMs accurately predict signal processing outcomes, recommend filtering strategies, or diagnose faults?
2. **Multi-Modal LLMs for Time-Series Data**: What tokenization strategy best preserves temporal and spectral information when converting time-series data for LLM consumption? What are the trade-offs between explicit feature extraction (MFCC, wavelet coefficients) and learned tokenization?
3. **Joint Signal-Text Architectures**: Can we design neural architectures that process raw signal segments and text tokens through a shared attention mechanism, enabling genuine multi-modal reasoning rather than late-fusion of independent predictions?

### Proposed Methods

- **Time-Series Tokenization**: Develop and compare tokenization strategies for time-series data including fixed-window discretization, adaptive segmentation, learnable codebooks (VQ-VAE), and spectral feature extraction
- **Multi-Modal Embeddings**: Train joint signal-text embedding spaces using contrastive learning, enabling semantic similarity search across modalities
- **Hybrid DSP-LLM Architectures**: Design and evaluate architectures that combine classical DSP preprocessing (FFT, wavelet transform) with transformer-based reasoning layers

### Application Domains

- **Industrial Fault Diagnosis**: LLM-powered analysis of vibration, acoustic, and electrical signatures for predictive maintenance
- **Biomedical Signal Interpretation**: LLM-assisted ECG/EEG analysis with natural language explanation of findings
- **Communications Intelligence**: LLM reasoning about RF channel characteristics for intelligent spectrum management
- **Environmental Monitoring**: Multi-modal LLM systems for integrating sensor streams with environmental knowledge bases

## 🔌 Area 4: Edge AI & TinyML

### Motivation

The most impactful AI deployments are often those closest to the physical world — embedded in industrial equipment, medical devices, agricultural sensors, and consumer electronics. These environments impose strict constraints on memory, compute, power, and connectivity that are fundamentally incompatible with cloud-based LLM deployment. The emerging field of TinyML and edge AI seeks to bring machine intelligence to these constrained environments, but the challenge intensifies dramatically for large language models.

### Research Questions

- What quantization and pruning strategies best preserve LLM reasoning capability when targeting 4GB RAM edge hardware?
- How should LLM architectures be modified to enable efficient streaming inference on ARM Cortex-A processors?
- What is the minimum viable model size and capability for useful natural language interfaces on IoT gateways?
- How can hardware-software co-design methodologies from embedded systems inform LLM architecture choices?

### Proposed Methods

- Systematic benchmarking of GPTQ, GGUF (Q4_K_M through Q8_0), and AWQ quantization methods on Raspberry Pi 4/5, Jetson Nano, and custom FPGA platforms
- Knowledge distillation from larger teacher models (Llama-3 70B) to smaller student models (1B–3B parameters) with domain-specific fine-tuning
- Hardware-aware neural architecture search (NAS) for edge LLM architectures optimized for specific hardware targets

### Contributions

- **EdgeLLM-Bench**: Benchmark suite for evaluating LLMs on edge hardware with standardized hardware targets and measurement methodology
- Open-source toolkit for automated LLM quantization and deployment on Raspberry Pi and Jetson platforms
- Published guidelines for hardware-aware LLM architecture design

## 💓 Area 5: Biomedical Signal Analysis

### Motivation

Healthcare represents one of the highest-impact application domains for AI, yet it is also one of the most demanding in terms of accuracy, interpretability, and reliability requirements. Biomedical signals — ECG, EEG, EMG, PPG, and medical imaging — contain rich diagnostic information that trained clinicians can extract but that automated systems have historically struggled to match in terms of both accuracy and explainability. The combination of deep learning for signal feature extraction with LLM-powered explanation generation offers a promising path toward AI-assisted clinical decision support that is both accurate and interpretable.

### Research Directions

- **ECG Analysis and Arrhythmia Detection**: CNN-LSTM architectures for multi-class arrhythmia classification on standard databases (MIT-BIH, PhysioNet), combined with LLM-powered generation of clinical-style interpretation reports
- **EEG Seizure Detection**: Real-time seizure detection from multi-channel EEG with explainable AI and LLM-generated clinical summaries
- **Medical Imaging + Report Generation**: Vision-language models for automated radiology report generation, with emphasis on factual grounding and hallucination prevention

## 🔭 Current Research Projects

### Project R-1: Domain-Adaptive LLM Benchmark for Engineering Sciences

**Abstract**: We are constructing **EngLLM-Bench**, a comprehensive evaluation benchmark for assessing large language model performance across four engineering subdisciplines: digital signal processing, embedded systems programming, RF communications, and circuit analysis. The benchmark comprises 2,400 expert-validated question-answer pairs spanning conceptual reasoning, mathematical derivation, code generation, and fault diagnosis tasks.

**Methodology**: Expert annotation (PhD-level engineers and faculty), stratified sampling across difficulty levels, contamination testing against existing LLM training data, automated evaluation pipeline using GPT-4-as-judge calibrated against human expert ratings.

**Status**: Dataset construction phase — 60% complete

**Expected Contributions**: (1) Publicly released benchmark dataset; (2) Comparative evaluation of 10+ leading LLMs on engineering tasks; (3) Analysis of the gap between general and domain-specific LLM capabilities; (4) Fine-tuning recommendations for engineering domain adaptation.

**Target Venue**: ACL 2026 / EMNLP 2026

---

### Project R-2: QLoRA Fine-tuning Analysis for Scientific Domains

**Abstract**: Systematic empirical analysis of QLoRA hyperparameter configurations (rank r ∈ {4, 8, 16, 32, 64}, alpha ∈ {16, 32, 64}, target modules, quantization precision) on domain adaptation of Llama-3 and Mistral-7B for scientific literature processing. We investigate the quality-efficiency frontier across GPU memory, training time, and downstream task performance dimensions.

**Methodology**: Controlled ablation studies on a curated scientific QA dataset; evaluation using exact match, ROUGE-L, BERTScore, and domain expert ratings; training on 4× A100 40GB GPUs.

**Status**: Pilot experiments complete; full ablation study in progress.

**Expected Contributions**: (1) Empirical guidelines for QLoRA configuration for scientific domain adaptation; (2) Analysis of catastrophic forgetting trade-offs; (3) Open-source fine-tuned model checkpoints.

**Target Venue**: ICLR 2026 / NeurIPS 2025 Workshop

---

### Project R-3: Time-Series Tokenization for Multi-Modal LLM Reasoning

**Abstract**: We investigate strategies for representing physical time-series signals as token sequences consumable by pre-trained LLMs, enabling joint signal-text reasoning without task-specific architecture modifications. We compare fixed-window discretization, adaptive segmentation, VQ-VAE learned codebooks, and spectral feature (MFCC, wavelet) extraction across industrial vibration, ECG, and IoT sensor benchmarks.

**Methodology**: Representation learning via contrastive training on signal-text pairs; downstream evaluation on signal QA, anomaly explanation, and fault diagnosis tasks; ablation of tokenization strategy, vocabulary size, and sequence length.

**Status**: Architecture design and initial implementation complete; training in progress.

**Expected Contributions**: (1) Novel tokenization framework for physical time-series; (2) Open-source time-series LLM toolkit; (3) Multi-modal benchmark for signal-text reasoning.

**Target Venue**: ICASSP 2026 / NeurIPS 2025

---

### Project R-4: EdgeLLM Deployment Benchmarking Suite

**Abstract**: Systematic benchmarking of quantized LLM inference on consumer and industrial edge hardware. We evaluate GGUF-quantized Llama-3 (1B, 3B, 8B) and Phi-3-mini variants across Raspberry Pi 4B (4GB), Raspberry Pi 5 (8GB), NVIDIA Jetson Nano, and Orange Pi 5 Pro platforms, measuring tokens/second throughput, first-token latency, memory footprint, and power consumption alongside quality preservation metrics (perplexity, task accuracy) relative to FP16 baseline.

**Methodology**: Automated benchmarking pipeline with controlled workloads (interactive QA, RAG-augmented QA, code completion); statistical analysis of quality-efficiency trade-offs; open-source benchmark harness.

**Status**: Hardware procurement complete; benchmarking pipeline implemented; data collection in progress.

**Expected Contributions**: (1) Publicly released benchmark results table; (2) Best-practice deployment guide for edge LLMs; (3) Open-source benchmarking toolkit.

**Target Venue**: MLSys 2026 / ISCA 2026
